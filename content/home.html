Welcome to FunG,

a C++-library for invariant-based modeling and the generation of complex functions, including first three derivatives and arbitrary (meaningful) variable types. The implementation is optimized for finite-element computations. It is motivated by the following ideas:

Generic Programming

FunG does not depend on specific types. Instead only a small interface must be satisfied (see Arithmetic Types). This interface is naturally fulfilled by built-in arithmetic types. Instead a small set of requirements, that is fulfilled by built-in arithmetic types, has been taken from mathematics (see HERE) to work with vectors, matrices and other objects one would possibly want to use. This is achieved with modern C++-features such as auto, decltype, std::declval and the incredible void_t (if you don't know it, watch this).

Invariant-based Modeling

Reasonable physical models require invariance with respect to the observer. For this different sets of invariants have been developed. FunG provides optimized implementations of modified and unmodified versions of principal and mixed invariants. With their complex invariant-based models can be implemented within few seconds.

Naming

The name FunG was chosen as a tribute to Yuan-Cheng Fung and his numerous contributions to the field of biomechanics. State-of-the-art models based on his exponential constitutive relation where the motivation to write this library.
